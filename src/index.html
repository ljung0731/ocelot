1. Summary

An ocelot is a wild cat, and its scientific name is Leopardus pardalis wich means “like a leopard”. Its average size is roughly 40 to 50 cm at shoulders and the weight is between 6.6 and 15.5kg. The life expectancy of an ocelot is 15.7 years, but it can live up to 20 years. It's founded in the southwestern US, Mexico, Central and South America, and the Caribbean islands of Trinidad and Margarita. It is the second largest spotted cats in South America. An ocelot is often threatened by traffic accidents, illegal fur trade, hunting, and habitant destruction. It is listed as least concern on the IUCN Red List, but its populations are decreasing. Back in the Aztec and Incan civilization, ocelots were associated with humans as pets. A famous painter, Salvador Dali, also had an ocelot as his pet. It is still legal to have the ocelot as a pet like a house cat in some places.

2. Characteristic

An ocelot is a medium-sized cat with black or dark brown doughnut-shaped dots with dark tan in the middle. The pattern is similar to a leopard. Their coat is very smooth and short, about 0.8 cm on the belly area, and 1cm on the back. Ocelots have a few dark stripes running through from the back of the neck to tails. They also have markings on their cheeks with stripes. Every ocelot has a unique color pattern. Their unique fur pattern can be used to identify one other. Ocelots have long ringed tails. The average tail length is 10 to 16 inches. An ocelot's weight at birth is around 7 to 12 ounces. Females weigh around 6.6 to 11.3kg and males are 8 to 15.5kg.

3. Distribution and Habitats

Ocelots are found in the southern part of Texas to northern part of Argentina in South America. In the US, ocelots are spotted in Texas, Arizona, Louisiana, and Arkansas. Ocelots live in tropical forests, thorn forest, mangrove swamps, and savannas. Ocelots desires habitats with good availability of water, and prey. Also, this area can be good for avoiding predators as well. They like areas with dense forest cover and water. If the area is too open space with no tree and bushes, they can't survive, so live to find other area to live. They tend to live a far from roads and villages. They also avoid steep slopes and highly elevated areas where it's hard to find preys. They normally appear at elevations below 1,200 m and have occasionally been recorded up to 3,800 m. Ocelots allocate large parts of their ranges with cougar, jaguarundi, jaguar, margay, and oncilla.

4. Life Style

Experts say that it is hard to study ocelots because they are active mostly at night, especially at dawn and dusk. When it is raining or cloudy in the daytime, they could be active in daytime. Ocelots sleep in bushes, on a tree brunch, or inside a hollow tree to be hidden from other predators during the daytime. Ocelots spend most of their time on the ground, but they can also swim well, climb, and jump high. Ocelots haunt mainly small rodents. It habitually preys on animals that weigh less than 1kg. They sometimes eat birds, snakes, iguanas, baby deer, rabbits, baby peccaries, and even fish and crabs. They tend to look for preys when they are strolling. They walk slowly to search preys. Time to time they may sit and wait for preys to appear. They can wait for prey for a half hour or even an hour at certain site. Ocelots usually eat prey immediately once it's killed, but they remove bird feathers before eating. Ocelots are smart enough to save their food. If they can't finish the carcass in one feeding, they cover the food and return the next night to finish the food.

5. Reproduction and Life cycle

Ocelots can mate any time of the year. However the peak mating seasons of them can be vary depending on their locations. For the ocelots in Texas, their mating season is typically in October to winter, and for ones in Central America, it is usually in spring months. Both male and female ocelots make short and long-range sounds. The gestational period of ocelots is 79 days to 85 days. Female ocelots normally give birth in dens vegetation area. A newborn kitten weighs around 200g to 340 g. A newborn ocelot has black or dark brown spots and stripes on gray background. A mother ocelot stays in the den about 13 to 64 days. The color of the fur changes from gray to golden brown as the ocelot grows older. Kittens opens their eyes 15 to 18 days after their born. Ocelots usually become independent after a year. However, they might stay with their mother for one more year. They start disappearing and forming their own territory. They typically live about 15.7 years, but it can live up to 20 years.
